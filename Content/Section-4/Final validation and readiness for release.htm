Final validation and readiness for release

Once testing and QA are complete, the validation of the readiness phase ensures the documentation is fully prepared for release. This is the final gate before deployment, confirming the completeness, correctness, and approval of localized content.

Readiness validation checklist

  1. Completion and coverage checks

    • Confirm that all required content is fully translated and reviewed.

    • Verify no missing strings or outdated source content in localized versions.

  2. Final QA pass (automated + manual)

    • Re-run automated tools to check spelling, grammar, tags, placeholders, and terminology.

    • Perform a final manual spot-check, focusing on high-visibility or user-critical areas.

  3. Contextual display and functionality

    • Check how the content appears in its final environment:

      • Text fit (no truncation, overflow).

      • Proper formatting of dates, currencies, and numbers.

      • Working links, accurate references, and correct visuals.

  4. Stakeholder review and sign-off

    • Conduct a last round of review with language leads, product owners, or localization managers.

    • Use a checklist or scorecard to guide and document decisions.

    • Capture sign-off from relevant stakeholders.

  5. Packaging and deployment verification

    • Ensure:

      • Files are properly named and placed in systems or repositories.

      • Encoding is preserved for non-Latin scripts.

      • Localized content is correctly linked to product builds or platforms.

  6. Documentation and reporting

    • Record:

      • Who signed off and when.

      • Any deferred issues and their rationale.

      • Lessons learned for future releases.

      • Updated guidelines, if applicable.

  7. Post-release monitoring (recommended)

    • Watch for user feedback, support tickets, or usage patterns in localized markets.

    • Log post-release issues and feed them back into the QA and localization process for continuous improvement.
