Validation of readiness

The validation of readiness process ensures that localized documentation is complete, accurate, functional, and approved for release. It’s the final gate before content is delivered to users—whether internally or publicly. It ensures the localized documentation is truly ready for its audience—not just technically “done.” While it can vary depending on the workflow, tools, and teams, the process generally follows these key steps:

Check completion and coverage

  • Confirm all content is translated and reviewed.

  • Ensure no strings or sections are missing in any language.

  • Verify that source updates have been correctly reflected in localized versions.

Run final QA checks (automated + manual)

  • Automated QA tools. Re-run spelling, grammar, placeholder integrity, tag structure, and terminology checks.

  • Manual review. Perform a final linguistic and functional spot-check, especially for high-impact or user-facing content.

Context and formatting review

Ensure localized content displays correctly in its final environment:

  • UI layout and text fit (no truncation, overflow, or misalignment).

  • Correct formatting of dates, numbers, currencies, etc.

  • Valid hyperlinks, cross-references, and visuals.

Stakeholder review and sign-off

  • Conduct a final review with key stakeholders, such as:

    • In-market reviewers or language leads.

    • Product managers.

    • Technical writers or localization managers.

  • Use a checklist or scorecard to guide the review and record sign-off decisions.

Verify packaging and delivery

Confirm that localized content is packaged and deployed correctly:

  • Files are named and placed correctly in the system or repo.

  • File encoding is preserved (especially for non-Latin scripts).

  • Localized content is linked to the correct product build or platform.

Document the validation outcome

  • Record:

    • Who signed off on what.

    • Any known issues deferred or accepted as-is.

    • Lessons learned or updates needed in style guides, glossaries, etc.

  • Store validation checklists, reports, and sign-off records for audit or reference.

Post-release monitoring (optional but recommended)

  • After release, keep an eye out for:

    • User feedback or support tickets in localized markets.

    • Reports of display, accuracy, or comprehension issues.

  • Feed this information back into your localization and QA processes.
