Text layout and formatting

Effective text layout and formatting are crucial for creating documents that are visually appealing, easy to read, and professionally presented.

Key aspects of text layout and formatting

Text direction. Support both left-to-right (LTR) languages (for example, English, Spanish) and right-to-left (RTL) languages (for example, Arabic, Hebrew). Ensure proper alignment and flow of text, UI elements, and navigation based on the language’s directionality.

Font support. Select fonts that support the glyphs and diacritics of target languages. For example, ensure fonts include Cyrillic, Chinese, or Arabic characters as needed.

Date, time, and number formats. Adapt formats to local conventions (for example, MM/DD/YYYY in the US vs DD/MM/YYYY in the UK; commas vs periods for decimal separators).

Currency and units. Display currency symbols, placement (for example, $100 vs 100 €), and measurement units (for example, metric vs imperial) according to local standards.

Sorting and collation. Implement language-specific sorting rules, as alphabetical order varies (for example, Spanish treats "ñ" as a separate letter; Chinese may sort by stroke count or pinyin).

Pluralization. Support language-specific plural rules (for example, English has singular/plural, while Arabic has dual and multiple plural forms).

Gender and grammatical agreement. Handle gender-specific text (for example, in French, adjectives change based on noun gender) and other grammatical nuances dynamically.

Accessibility. Ensure text layout supports screen readers and other assistive technologies, with proper language tagging (for example, HTML lang attributes).

Dynamic UI adjustments. Enable adaptable layouts (for example, resizable containers) to respond to text changes without disrupting the design.

Locale-specific formatting. Apply locale rules for capitalization, punctuation, and quotation styles (for example, " “ vs « »).

​Best practices of text layout and formatting

Use appropriate line length. Limit line length to around 50-75 characters to enhance readability and prevent reader fatigue.

Incorporate visual aids. Use images, charts, and other visual elements to complement the text and aid in conveying information effectively. ​

Ensure high contrast. Maintain sufficient contrast between text and background colors to improve readability, especially for users with visual impairments. ​

Use white space. Incorporate adequate white space around text blocks to prevent clutter and improve focus. Adequate white space improves readability, emphasizes key elements, and enhances the overall aesthetic appeal.

Avoid overuse of formatting styles. Use bold, italics, and underlining sparingly to avoid clutter and maintain the document's professional appearance. ​

Test print layouts. Before finalizing, print a draft to ensure that the layout, margins, and colors appear as intended in the physical format.
